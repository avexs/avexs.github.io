Personal Website

The purpose of this project was to learn more about web development and the importance of creating a personal brand. I purposely avoided using a template when I began this project, since I wanted to have the full experience of designing and programming a responsive website. I knew I would not learn as much if I had chosen a template site; and although the aesthetics of the site maybe be lacking as a result of this decision, the payoff was completely worth it.

If you are interested in viewing the source and bugs for this site, please click on the computer icon above to visit the github for this project.

RGB Rubix Cube

Three-dimensional rubix cube using RBG leds and an Arduino

This project began when I attended my very first hackathon, for which I unfortunately showed up on the last day and was unable to finish in time. The idea for this project was to create arrays of leds which would act as each face of the cube. Using switches, the user would be able to rotate portions of the cube. The plan for this project was to first implement the cube using an arduino as the logic controller, and to then later syntehsize the project using gate-level logic.

About Me

Who Am I?

I am currently a Junior at Case Western Reserve University. My major is Computer Engineering, since my interests extend heavily into both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering; with my passion lying in digital hardware.

I am a proud brother of Phi Kappa Theta, Ohio Alpha Beta at Case. The brotherhood has become a very large part of my life ever since I joined. I have learned to be confident in who I am, and I have been pushed to take multiple leadership positions which I would have never considered previously. For my Junior year I will be taking a leadership postion on the executive board as the Vice President of Fraternity.

I am also on the executive board of the Longboarding club at Case as the Public Relations Chair. And although these leadership positions take up much of my freetime, I also continue to persue my other hobbies. I think of myself as an amateur artist. I have spent the last couple of years practicing my skills in drawing, painting, design, and more recently photography. When I end up spending several days finishing various homework assignments and studying to master concepts, I find relief in the fact that I can sit down and sketch whatever comes to my mind, or explore the world and shoot a roll of film.
